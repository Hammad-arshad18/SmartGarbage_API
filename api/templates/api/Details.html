Summary

Pakistan, with a population of 227 million, is the world's fifth-most populous country. The heap of garbage is a common sight in the urban areas of Pakistan. Mismanagement of garbage management causes soil erosion, air, and water contamination which ultimately creates health and environmental hazards for the citizens of Pakistan. Diarrhea, pneumonia, and the release of methane are a few examples of these hazards. It is, therefore, important to devise an efficient mechanism for waste management. With the help of modern and emerging technologies, the concerned authorities can reform the process of waste management in the right direction in Pakistan. This project proposes a comprehensive solution for waste management. It is a software and hardware-based solution that will facilitate municipal corporations, residents of urban areas, and other concerned authorities for the effective management of waste. To reap the benefits of an integrated system for waste management, this project utilizes the concepts of the Internet of Things (IoT) and Deep Learning. The system will be a hybrid application (i.e., usage of web and mobile-based technologies). It will identify the piles of garbage on the streets and the roads. For this purpose, sensors such as cameras will be installed in the various locations of a city. These cameras will send images to a web server through microcontrollers where a deep learning-based trained model will identify the presence of the piles of garbage. After the identification of the garbage, the system will generate alerts for the authorities and janitorial staff of that area. It will also send the exact location of the piles of garbage to the mentioned authorities and the staff. The tasks of the waste collection can be assigned automatically by identifying the janitorial staff on duty. On the other hand, the head of the janitorial staff can also assign a task if needed. The system keeps checking the status of the assigned task (if the piles of garbage are collected or not) after the interval set by the head. After the task completion, it generates a report. Moreover, the system also keeps an eye on the performance of the staff in each area. It also identifies the staff of the month and the year which are selected on the basis of the cleanliness of the assigned area. The residents of an area can also lodge complaints regarding the cleanliness of their areas through the mobile application. Consequently, making a contribution to maintaining cleanliness in their city. In short, the proposed system will bring the stakeholders in one place for achieving the sustainable goal of a neat and clean Pakistan.

Project Objectives

The primary objective is to resolve the issue of improper waste management in Pakistan. For this purpose, the proposed solution will bring all the stakeholders on one platform where they will work together for achieving the mentioned goal. The deep-learning-based intelligent algorithm will help in the identification of heaps of garbage in the city. Moreover, it will also improve the performance of janitorial staff and the concerned authorities by evaluating their performance. In addition to this, based on the system-generated reports, the government officials can perceive the future needs and consequently, plan for waste management in the future. Resultantly, creating an environment and climate-friendly happy lifestyle for the citizens of Pakistan.

Project Implementations Details

The proposed system is a hybrid application, i.e., web and mobile-based. It is noteworthy that the web application is developed using the Django REST framework which is a Python-based web open-source framework that follows the model-template-views architectural pattern. The mobile application is a cross-platform application that is developed using Flutter. It is an open-source UI software development kit created by Google. For database management, MySQL (for users’ and waste management records), Firebase (for generating alerts), and SQLite (for keeping the records of tasks and complaints on the mobile users’ side). In the first phase, the system will keep monitoring the status of the roads and the streets where cameras are installed. For this purpose, the ESP 32 cameras will be connected to the webserver through Raspberry Pi 4B microcontrollers. The captured images will be sent to a web server where the piles of garbage will be identified with the help of a trained deep-learning model in the second phase. YOLO algorithm is a strong candidate for this purpose. The training will be carried out using various datasets of the piles of garbage. The trained model will label the piles of garbage. As soon as the pile of garbage is identified by the system, it will generate alerts for the authorities through web and mobile applications. The alerts will be generated for those staff members who will be on duty on that day and time. For this purpose, the concerned authorities and the janitorial staff will be registered on the system. The head of each area will be able to enter the working days of the staff. The system will have the capability to assign the task of collecting the garbage to a staff member who is available in a nearby area. In addition to this, the head of janitorial staff may also assign the task manually. The system will keep monitoring the place where the pile of the garbage is identified. As soon as the area is cleaned, it will change the task status as completed. If the task is not completed within the stipulated time, the system will send an alert to the higher management of that area. Moreover, it will keep monitoring the performance of the staff on a monthly and yearly basis. The system will also consider the number of complaints lodged by the residents of the area. The waste management authority may assign a staff member to deal with the complaints of the residents. A comprehensive report regarding the resolution of the complaints will also be generated for the higher authorities. It will also generate a report regarding the cleanliness of the area on a monthly and yearly basis.

It will be achieved by identifying and collecting the piles of garbage in the city. Moreover, it will improve the efficiency and the performance of the authorities and the staff involved in the waste management by monitoring and evaluating their record
